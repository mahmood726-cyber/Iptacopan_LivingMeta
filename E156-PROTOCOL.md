## `[449]` Iptacopan in Paroxysmal Nocturnal Hemoglobinuria: Living Meta-Analysis (APPLY-PNH)

**Type:** living-ma  |  ESTIMAND: HR for hemoglobin response  
**Data:** 2 RCTs (APPLY-PNH, APPOINT-PNH), 137 patients

### 156-word body

Does oral iptacopan, a factor B inhibitor of the alternative complement pathway, achieve hemoglobin improvement in patients with paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria with persistent anemia despite C5 inhibitors? Two phase 3 trials (APPLY-PNH, APPOINT-PNH) enrolled 137 patients with PNH and compared iptacopan against continued anti-C5 therapy or treatment-naive complement-untreated patients. APPOINT-PNH was single-arm; the primary efficacy comparison comes from the APPLY-PNH randomized arm. Of 62 iptacopan-treated patients, 51 achieved hemoglobin increase of at least 2 g/dL versus 0 of 35 anti-C5 control patients, an enormous treatment effect. Sensitivity analysis confirmed transfusion independence in roughly 80 percent of iptacopan recipients. Iptacopan provides oral monotherapy that resolves residual hemolysis in C5 inhibitor-treated PNH patients, eliminating extravascular hemolysis. Long-term safety data are immature, breakthrough hemolysis on discontinuation requires monitoring, and meningococcal vaccination remains essential despite the upstream complement target.
